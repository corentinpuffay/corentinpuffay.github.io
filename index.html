About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at KU Leuven with a strong interest in neuroscience and artificial intelligence. I am affiliated with the ExpORL group (Prof. Tom Francart) and the PSI group (Prof. Hugo Van Hamme).

My research focuses on modeling language processing in the human brain by linking speech representations to brain responses measured through EEG and invasive recordings. I also leverage large language models to generate speech representations, capturing aspects of language processing similar to those in the human brain.

In June 2025, I obtained my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering & Biomedical Sciences, focusing on relating continuous speech to brain signals using deep learning architectures, ranging from convolutional networks to transformers. I also apply my models to study speech processing in different populations, including aphasia and epilepsy.
