Corentin Puffay

Neuroscience & AI Researcher

I am a postdoctoral researcher at KU Leuven with a strong interest in Neuroscience and Artificial Intelligence. I am affiliated with both the ExpORL group (Prof. Tom Francart) and the PSI group (Prof. Hugo Van hamme). My research focuses on modeling language processing in the human brain by training machine learning models to link speech representations with brain responses measured through Electroencephalography (EEG) and invasive recordings. Additionally, I leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate speech representations, as they appear to capture aspects of language processing similar to those in the human brain. I visited the lab of Prof. Prof. Nima Mesgarani at Columbia University in June 2024.

In June 2025, I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering & Biomedical Sciences, focusing on relating continuous speech to brain signals (EEG, and invasive recordings) using novel deep learning architectures ranging from convolutional networks to transformers. After developing models using different speech representations, reflecting language and auditory processing, I could leverage my models to investigate speech processing in different populations (e.g., aphasia and epilepsy).
